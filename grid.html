MARK JOHNSON JD

Forensic Arts & Animation

Mr. Johnson received his bachelor's degree cum laude from Kansas State University in Manhattan, Kansas in 1975. He was awarded the Putnam Scholarship and inducted into Phi Kappa Phi honor society. He next attended the University of Kansas School of Law in Lawrence, Kansas where he received his Juris Doctorate degree in 1978.

As a practicing plaintiff’s trial lawyer through the 1980's, Mr. Johnson developed innovative uses of film, video and graphics for litigation and mediation. As computer graphics evolved, he acquired skills in the creation and manipulation of three-dimensional computer models. In 1992 a 3D animation of a complicated piece of industrial equipment created by Johnson assisted a jury in finding liability and awarding over 17 million dollars to the families of three workmen injured in a plant explosion.
